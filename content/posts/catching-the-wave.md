## Goal of this post

- Reflect on why I didn't go straight into CS and AI, starting from 2017 — the year Transformers came out and the year I started college.
- In hindsight, I obviously should have. But it wasn't obvious back then.
- Think through what long-term vision really means.
- Figure out: how do I get into a fast-growing field I care about *early*?
- If I had to redo the past nine years, what principles would I stand by to prepare for the future?
- The whole point is to consolidate lessons and improve myself going forward. What happened already happened.

## Inspirations

### Anthropic's success

- They didn't start until a few years ago. Now valued at $380B, going IPO this year, good news basically every day, exponential growth.
- I care about Anthropic the most because it's the most AI-safety-focused lab. A lot of the great safety researchers are there.
- Hard not to feel a sense of missing out. How great would it have been if I'd gotten into the AI space, and into Anthropic, two or three years ago when I graduated from my master's program.

### Early safety researchers

- Many successful safety researchers were not doing safety research until very recently — in a lot of cases four or five years ago, or even two or three years ago.
- Because they got in early, they're very successful now. Obviously they're very talented people and they deserve it, without a doubt.
- I want to be like that. I wish I could have gotten into this space earlier.

## My situation — an objective look

### Starting point (2017)

- I'm from China, which was not a frontier for machine learning. There was no frontier-level industry, and nobody was talking about safety at all.
- I was fresh out of high school. I was basically a kid who knew nothing. I didn't even speak English.

### The past few years — survival mode

- During my master's at USC, I was struggling to find a job.
- At Tesla, there was a layoff, and I went back on the job market again.
- For the majority of the past few years, I've been in survival mode.

### The turning point

- I realized the importance of AI safety very shortly after I joined Adobe.
- Given my situation, that was pretty much already the fastest possible route I could take.
